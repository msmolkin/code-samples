The terrors of hosting your own website are vast

Exacerbated by trying to style your website and make it look professional from scratch with nothing but a text editor and a web browser to check your work

Posted by Michael S. on August 20, 2019

Have you ever tried to host your own website? Have you tried it without all the tools provided by a content management system such as Wordpress and website builders/hosting providers/DNS performance services like Squarespace, Cloudflare, etc.? The more you strip away, the harder it becomes to do what you love. But until you learn what you are doing, adding too much can cost you.

I now know the most important piece of information: it's better to use it. If your project goes directly to the end-user, it can have as much overhead as all hell and it'll still work without any problems. Once you learn what you're doing, you can streamline it. Even though I learned everything before using it, I found out I have to streamline the process anyway, so I should've just learned as I went along. Would've saved a ton of time.

So maybe I wouldn't have gotten so lucky and the site or service would have had problems. But people understand. There's something I never knew. As long as you're providing a valuable service, people understand and love you, even if you have some slight hiccups along the way. You just need to communicate. As an aside, I'll need to keep this in mind as I go forward. Ship and then fix, don't fix and then (never) ship.
